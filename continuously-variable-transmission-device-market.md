<p><p>A Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT) device is an automotive transmission system that provides seamless acceleration by continuously adjusting the gear ratio without fixed steps. <p><p>The Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT) market includes three main types: Liquid, Electric, and Mechanical Transmission. Liquid Transmission uses hydraulic systems to transfer power, offering smooth operation and high efficiency. Electric Transmission relies on electric motors and controllers, providing precise control and energy recovery, ideal for hybrid and electric vehicles. Mechanical Transmission employs belts, pulleys, or chains for seamless gear ratio adjustments, ensuring fuel efficiency and adaptability. Each type caters to specific applications, balancing performance, efficiency, and cost in automotive and industrial sectors.</p></p>
