<p><p>The cryogenic insulation media market comprises various types, including polyurethane (PU) foam, cellular glass, polystyrene, fiberglass, and perlite. PU foam offers flexibility and durability, while cellular glass provides excellent thermal resistance and is chemically inert. Polystyrene is lightweight and cost-effective, whereas fiberglass offers high tensile strength. Perlite, an expanded volcanic glass, has low thermal conductivity. Additional materials like aerogels and elastomer foams are also utilized for their superior insulating properties in specialized applications, enhancing thermal efficiency in cryogenic environments.</p></p>
