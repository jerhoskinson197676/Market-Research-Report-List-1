<p><p>Incremental linear encoders are categorized into three primary types based on their sensing technology: mechanical, optical, and magnetic. Mechanical encoders use physical contact with a scale, offering high durability but limited resolution. Optical encoders rely on light interruption or reflection, providing high precision and resolution, making them suitable for fine measurements. Magnetic encoders utilize magnetic fields to detect position changes, offering robustness against contaminants and vibration, ideal for harsh environments. Each type serves distinct applications based on specific requirements.</p></p>
