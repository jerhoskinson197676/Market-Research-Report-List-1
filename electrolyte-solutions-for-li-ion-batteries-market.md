<p><p>Electrolyte solutions for lithium-ion batteries are categorized into liquid and solid electrolytes. Liquid electrolytes, typically composed of lithium salts dissolved in organic solvents, facilitate lithium-ion transport and enable high conductivity. They are widely used in commercial batteries but may pose safety risks due to flammability. Solid electrolytes, on the other hand, are composed of solid materials that conduct lithium ions, offering enhanced safety, stability, and potentially higher energy densities, making them increasingly attractive for next-generation battery technologies.</p></p>
