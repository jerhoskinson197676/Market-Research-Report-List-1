<p><p>The Nickel Powder market for Multi-Layer Ceramic Capacitor (MLCC) electrodes is primarily characterized by two production methods: Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) and Physical Vapor Deposition (PVD). CVD involves chemical reactions that deposit nickel on substrates, providing high purity and uniformity, essential for MLCC performance. PVD, on the other hand, uses physical processes to vaporize nickel, allowing for controlled thickness and morphology. Both methods cater to the growing demand for efficient, high-capacity MLCCs in electronics.</p></p>
