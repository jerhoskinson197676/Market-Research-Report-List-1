<p><p>The man-made regenerated cellulose fibres market primarily consists of two types: Viscose and Lyocell fibres. Viscose fibres are produced from wood pulp through a chemical process, resulting in a versatile, affordable material widely used in textiles and garments. Lyocell fibres, on the other hand, utilize a more eco-friendly process involving a closed-loop system that recycles solvents, leading to a stronger, biodegradable product. Both types cater to the increasing demand for sustainable and high-performance textiles in fashion and industrial applications.</p></p>
