<p><p>The anti-hepatitis virus drugs market comprises three main types. Nucleos(t)ide analogues, such as Lamivudine and Tenofovir, act by mimicking viral nucleotides to inhibit replication. Direct-acting antiviral agents (DAAs) target specific viral proteins to prevent hepatitis virus replication and are known for their efficacy. Interferon Alfa, an immune-modulating therapy, enhances the body's antiviral response but may have more side effects. Together, these therapies offer diverse treatment options for hepatitis infections, catering to varying patient needs.</p></p>
