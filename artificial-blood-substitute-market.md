<p><ul><li>Perfluorocarbon-based Oxygen Carrier (PFBOC)</li><li>Hemoglobin-based Oxygen Carrier (HBOC)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The artificial blood substitute market includes two main types: Perfluorocarbon-based Oxygen Carriers (PFBOC) and Hemoglobin-based Oxygen Carriers (HBOC). PFBOCs utilize perfluorocarbons to dissolve and transport oxygen, offering benefits such as prolonged shelf life and reduced risk of transfusion reactions. In contrast, HBOCs are derived from hemoglobin, aiming to mimic the oxygen-carrying function of human blood. Both types are being explored for various applications, including trauma care and surgical procedures, to address blood shortage and enhance patient outcomes.</p></p>
